The Brief

A wearable that disappears
into daily life.

Most upper-limb exosuits were built for industry and medicine — bulky, intrusive, hard to wear alone. Sleev is designed for the rhythm of independent living: worn through a hug, a phone call, a quiet morning.

기존의 상지 보조 로보틱스는 산업과 의료 현장을 위해 만들어져서 부피가 크고, 거추장스럽고, 혼자 착용하기 어려웠습니다.
Sleev는 자립적인 일상을 위해 디자인되었습니다. 누군가를 안을 때, 전화를 받을 때, 조용한 아침에도 함께합니다.

Sensors read intent through muscle expansion. The form sits close to the body. The strap fastens with one hand. The whole machine is shaped to be present without being seen.

FMG센서는 근육의 팽창을 통해 움직임의 의도를 읽고, 형태는 신체에 자연스럽게 밀착됩니다.

01   Sensing

Reads muscle expansion,
not skin signals.

FMG (Force Myography) interprets movement through pressure as muscles expand — paired with IMU joint tracking for a stable read on intent.

No fragile sEMG calibration. No medical setup. The wearer simply moves, and Sleev moves with them.

SensorsIMU + FMG
SizesS / M / L
02   Form

Shaped to vanish
against the body.

Inner arms minimise interference when holding a child or carrying a bag. The elbow stays clear when the arm bends and moves backward.

Quietly aesthetic, structurally invisible — a wearable that layers cleanly over an everyday outfit.

Range−5° → 140°
LayerOver innerwear
03   Daily

Built around the
rhythm of one hand.

A charging cradle holds Sleev steady so it can be put on with a single hand — no help required. Voice commands trigger frequent routines.

A rehabilitation mode reverses the actuator force to provide resistance, returning users toward their original daily lives.

The Architecture

Quiet machinery,
sized for the body.

Designed against the 2021 Size Korea ergonomic dataset, Sleev fits the 5th to 95th percentile of users in three sizes — a detachable sensor module, a soft fabric inner, a rigid outer shell that flexes only where it should.

사이즈코리아 인체측정 데이터를 기준으로, Sleev는 세 가지 사이즈로 제안되어 5~95% 분포의 사용자를 수용합니다. 분리형 센서 모듈, 부드러운 패브릭 이너, 그리고 필요한 곳에서만 유연하게 움직이는 견고한 외관으로 구성됩니다.

Form follows feeling

Soft where it touches.
Strong where it matters.

Three quiet capabilities.

Sleev is built around the small frictions of one-handed life — and the moments where a little assistance changes the day.

Sleev는 작은 도움이 하루를 바꾸는, 더 나은 일상의 순간들을 위해 만들어졌습니다.

Easy wear, one hand.

Sleev rests on its charging cradle while you slip your arm in and secure the strap — no second hand needed, no help required.

Sleev는 충전 크래들 위에 놓인 채로 팔을 끼우고 스트랩을 채울 수 있습니다. 다른 손도, 누군가의 도움도 필요하지 않습니다.

Voice action commands.

Frequent routines can be recorded and triggered by voice, reducing the need to manually re-instruct the robot through every familiar task.

반복되는 일상 동작은 음성으로 실행할 수 있어, 익숙한 동작마다 매번 다시 조작할 필요가 줄어듭니다.

Rehabilitation mode.

By reversing the direction of actuator force, Sleev becomes a resistance training partner — guiding users back toward their original daily lives.

액추에이터의 힘을 반대 방향으로 작동시키면 Sleev는 재활 트레이닝 파트너가 됩니다. 사용자가 본래의 일상으로 돌아갈 수 있도록 도움을 줍니다.

The Making

From sketch to
silent machine.

Sleev passed through dozens of strap configurations, foam mock-ups, and sensor housings before settling into its final form. The film below traces the work from first volume study to the body it wears today.

Process Film · 2024–2025
01

Volume study.

Foam blocks and tape mock-ups tested how much mass the upper arm could carry without becoming visually heavy.

02

Strap geometry.

Six strap configurations were trialled to find one that secures with a single hand and stays clear of the elbow.

03

Sensor housing.

FMG modules went through six iterations — from raw boards to the final flush-mounted, fabric-backed cell.

04

Daily-life test.

Wearers carried bags, lifted children, used a vacuum and a hairdryer — every scenario fed back into the form.

Prototype lineup
2024 — 2025 · Eight iterations
Left to right: structural frame studies, soft-shell mock-ups, sensor module revisions, and the final detachable FMG cell. Below, the woven inner strap and three fabric inner samples.
Studio documentation
Mock-up photography · Behind the scenes
Each prototype was photographed under controlled studio lighting — shot, re-shot, and re-staged across multiple sessions to document the physical evolution of the form before final production.
Scenario testing
AI-generated imagery · Concept validation
Eight everyday scenarios were rendered as AI-generated images and short video clips — holding a hairdryer, lifting a vacuum, carrying a child, putting on a coat, working at a table — and used to stress-test the form, strap geometry, and elbow clearance before physical wear trials.
The 60th Korea International Design Award · 2025

Awarded the
Presidential Grand Prize.

Sleev was awarded the highest honour at the 60th Korea International Design Award (2025) — selected from 6,611 entries across the Korean design industry. The work was recognised for proposing a quiet, daily-life rethinking of the upper-limb exosuit, where there had previously only been industrial and medical machines.

Sleev는 제60회 대한민국디자인전람회(2025)에서 6,611개 출품작 중 대상(대통령상)으로 선정되었습니다. 산업·의료 분야에 머물러 있던 상지 로보틱스를 일상의 관점에서 재해석한 부분이 좋은 평가를 받았습니다.

The award ceremony was held on November 14, 2025 at COEX Hall D as part of Design Korea 2025.

시상식은 2025년 11월 14일 COEX에서 진행되었습니다.
